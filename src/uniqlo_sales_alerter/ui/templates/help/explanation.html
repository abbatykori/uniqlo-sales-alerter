Explanation

How and why.

How matching works

The matcher loads every enabled, non-snoozed saved filter from SQLite, then evaluates each product against every filter. A product matches a filter when its gender is in the filter's set (UNISEX always passes), the discount is at or above min_discount, at least one in-stock size matches one of the filter's size lists, and availability_mode agrees with the product's storeStockOnly flag. Per-filter ignored_keywords drop matches on substring presence in the product name.

Each emitted deal lists every filter that matched it in matched_filter_ids. Notifications surface those names as chips.

How the heatmap works

Each matched deal writes one row to deal_observations with is_deep = 1 when discount meets the configured threshold. The 7×24 grid is a SQL GROUP BY strftime('%w', observed_at), strftime('%H', observed_at) over a rolling lookback (default 90 days). Opacity scales linearly with the cell's count relative to the max in the window. The grid stays hidden until 14 distinct days of data have accrued to avoid false "no deals on Tuesdays" signals.

Why saved filters (and not profiles)

One household, one buyer, many sizes. "Me tops" + "Me bottoms" + "Kid 5y" + "Spouse" is the real shape. Profiles imply multi-user accounts; filters are just saved queries — leaner, no auth.
